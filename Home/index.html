Top Treks in Maharashtra

1. Garbett Point

Garbett Point is located near Matheran with grand view of the plateau below. The huge plateau gives a view to various waterfalls on the neighboring mountains. Garbett Point is a place from where enthralling views of sunrise and sunset can be observed. Climate here is very cool with some breath taking views.

Altitude: 2,625 ft.
Difficulty Level: Moderate.
Trail Type: Initial trail is a dirt road. Then well defined through fields.

2. Harishchandragad Fort

Harishchandragad is one of the most challenging treks in Maharashtra. The main attraction of this hill fort - an almost 1,800 feet high concave fall is the Konkan cliff (Konkan Kada). It is a vertical overhang, like a cobra’s hood, providing a panoramic view of the surrounding hills. The weather conditions over here can get extremely windy!

Altitude: 4,665 ft.
Difficulty: Easy or Moderate or Difficult depending on the route
Trail Type: Well defined through fields & forests.

3. Kalsubai Peak

Kalsubai Peak, at an elevation of 5400 feet and with the title of ‘the Everest of Maharashtra’ is one of the most tough treks in Maharashtra. You will cross beautiful streams and pools, and see picturesque views of valleys and surrounding peaks. This is one of the top treks in Maharashtra for those looking for a fun challenge.

Altitude: 5,400 ft.
Difficulty: Moderate.
Trail Type: Well defined through meadows, forests, with rock steps in some places and iron ladders in others.

4. Visapur & Lohagad Fort

Visapur is a trek favoured among beginner trekker for the waterfalls along the trail. The trail begins from Bhaje village which is easily accessible owning to its proximity to Mumbai-Pune express highway.

Altitude: 3,389 ft.
Difficulty: Easy.
Trail Type: Well defined trail with boulders and steps all the way to the fort.

Click Here for Location

5. Devkund Trek

The Devkund trek is an ideal place for photography, for swimming in the pool, or for exploring the area around. It takes about 2 hours to trek from the base village and through the forest to reach Devkund waterfalls.

Altitude: 2,000 ft.
Difficulty: Easy to Moderate.
Trail Type: Flat walk with a few, short stretches of gradual ascent.
